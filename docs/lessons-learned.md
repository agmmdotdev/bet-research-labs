# Lessons Learned

This file records practical lessons from reviewed matches. The purpose is to improve market selection over time.

## 1. Correct match shape is not always correct market selection

Belgium vs New Zealand showed the difference clearly.

The match profile suggested New Zealand could score but might not defend well for 90 minutes. That profile was better for **BTTS Yes** or **Over** than for **New Zealand +2.5 handicap**.

Lesson:

> If the underdog has attacking threat but a weak defensive line, prefer BTTS/Over instead of relying on the underdog to hold a handicap.

## 2. A skipped winner is not automatically a mistake

Colombia double chance / +0.5 was a good value read because Colombia only needed to avoid defeat, had strong tactical incentives, and had a favorable crowd environment in Miami.

Skipping it because the risk felt uncomfortable was not necessarily bad discipline. A forced bet made without conviction can be worse than a skipped winner.

Lesson:

> Confidence and risk tolerance matter. Track skipped picks, but do not chase future bets because a skipped pick won.

## 3. Venue demographics can matter in so-called neutral matches

Colombia vs Portugal highlighted that a neutral venue can function like a home-leaning venue when one team has major diaspora support.

This can affect:

- pressing intensity,
- emotional momentum,
- referee pressure,
- confidence after chances,
- late-match energy.

Lesson:

> Venue/fan split should be a formal checklist item for World Cup matches in the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

## 4. Lineup risk should change price thresholds, not automatically kill a bet

Argentina vs Jordan created a useful tension. Messi had scored all of Argentina’s previous tournament goals and was expected to start on the bench. That made Argentina team total Over 2.5 riskier.

However, Jordan’s defensive weakness and Argentina’s attacking depth still made the Over 2.5 capable of landing.

Lesson:

> Star absence or rotation should reduce stake or require higher odds. It does not automatically mean the original angle is wrong.

## 5. Asian handicap lines require settlement discipline

Argentina -2.00 Asian Handicap and Argentina -2.25 Asian Handicap are not equivalent.

- **-2.00**: a two-goal win is a push.
- **-2.25**: a two-goal win is a half loss.

Lesson:

> Do not accept a worse Asian handicap line without demanding a better price.

## 6. Team incentives can beat team reputation

Colombia vs Portugal was not simply a question of whether Portugal had the stronger squad. Colombia had a draw-friendly incentive structure, while Portugal had to push harder if they wanted top spot.

Lesson:

> Bet the market that matches the incentives, not the more famous team.

## 7. Track result quality and process quality separately

A bet can win for the wrong reason, and a bet can lose despite a good read. The log should record both.

Suggested labels:

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Good process / won | Best case. Reasoning and result aligned. |
| Good process / lost | Acceptable variance or late event. Review but do not overreact. |
| Bad process / won | Dangerous. Do not reinforce the bad reasoning. |
| Bad process / lost | Clear correction needed. |
| Unverified | Result or closing odds not yet logged. |
