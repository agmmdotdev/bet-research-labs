# Bankroll and Risk Rules

This file exists to prevent good research from turning into poor staking decisions.

## Core rules

1. Use flat staking by default.
2. Do not increase stake size because of a short winning streak.
3. Do not chase losses.
4. Do not parlay correlated bets unless the correlation is intentionally priced and understood.
5. Track skipped picks as well as placed picks. A skipped winner is not automatically a mistake.
6. Judge the process over a long sample, ideally 50 to 100 tracked picks or more.

## Recommended unit model

A **unit** should be a fixed percentage of bankroll, not a random emotional stake.

Conservative setup:

| Confidence tier | Stake |
|---|---:|
| Lean only | 0 units / watch only |
| Standard pick | 1 unit |
| Strong pick | 1.25 units max |
| Very rare top edge | 1.5 units max |

Avoid going above 1.5 units until the model has a long tracked history.

## Why flat staking matters

A short hot streak can happen from good reasoning, luck, or both. Increasing stake size too quickly creates the risk of giving back profit when variance turns.

The target is not to win every match. The target is to repeatedly take prices that are better than the true probability.

## Closing-line value

Where possible, record the odds when the bet was recommended and the closing odds before kickoff.

If a pick is taken at 1.90 and closes at 1.70, that is usually a strong sign the read had value, regardless of whether the match result won or lost.

## Stop rules

Use stop rules before the betting session starts:

- Maximum daily loss: 2 to 3 units.
- Maximum number of bets per day: pre-defined, not emotional.
- No extra stake after a late bad beat.
- No new bet without a written reason and price threshold.

## Public-repo note

Do not store sensitive personal bankroll amounts, account balances, or betting-site credentials in this repository.
